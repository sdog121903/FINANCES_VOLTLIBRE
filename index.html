09 / INFLECTION POINTS

Where Costs Jump & Where Profit Accelerates

1PRO USER

First Break-Even Point

A single Pro subscriber at €300/month covers all €237 in fixed monthly costs with €57.80 remaining. This is the day-1 target. With even 1 Pro user, every Academy user added is pure upside with near-zero marginal cost. Priority action: acquire the first Pro user before any other growth metric.

~50USERS

Vercel Hobby → Pro ($20/mo)

When the platform has 50+ registered users in production, upgrade Vercel to the Pro plan ($20/month ≈ €19). The Hobby tier has no SLA, can rate-limit deployments, and is not intended for commercial production. At €19/month, Vercel Pro provides unlimited projects, CI/CD pipelines, and 1TB bandwidth. This upgrade should happen the moment the first real paying user signs up, not at the 50-user threshold.

~200USERS

Supabase Free → Pro ($25/mo)

The Supabase free tier supports 50,000 MAUs and 500MB database — but the critical risk is the project pause on inactivity. Any 7-day period without usage will cause a production outage. At ~200 active users generating daily traffic this risk is mitigated, but best practice is to move to Supabase Pro ($25/month ≈ €23) when the first paying user exists. The Pro tier also provides daily automated backups — critical for a financial platform handling agent earnings and invoice data.

~1KUSERS

Gestoría Complexity Threshold

At ~1,000 Academy users, the Spanish gestoría is managing potentially 1,500+ commission payouts per month across multiple energy providers with varying payment terms. A basic online gestoría (€130/mo) may no longer be sufficient. Budget for an upgrade to a dedicated accounting firm (€200–€400/month) that specializes in digital platforms and multi-party payout reconciliation. This is the only significant operational cost inflection point in the model.

5KUSERS

The True Scalability Proof Point

At 5,000 users (80/20 mix), monthly revenue is €534,000 with total costs of only €5,889. The cost-to-revenue ratio is 1.1%. This is the defining characteristic of a well-architected SaaS platform: revenue scales linearly with users, while costs scale logarithmically. At 5,000 users, every additional user generates ~€107/month in net profit contribution with under €1 in marginal cost.

🏦 Permanent Establishment (PE) Risk — Most Important Tax Issue VoltLibre is a Wyoming LLC but its users, customers, and revenue sources are all in Spain. Spanish tax authorities (AEAT) may assert that VoltLibre has a "permanent establishment" in Spain, which would subject its Spanish-source income to Spanish Corporation Tax (25%). This risk is material. The mitigation strategies — ensuring the LLC has no employees in Spain, that all operations are digital and the owner is not physically present conducting business in Spain — should be reviewed with a tax lawyer experienced in US–Spain structures before the platform generates significant revenue. Budget for a one-time legal opinion: €800–€2,000.
